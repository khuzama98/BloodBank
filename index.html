Giving blood is a quick and enriching way to give back to your community..

By donating blood, you can help families who have been in accidents or experienced trauma, mothers experiencing labor complications, fathers having heart surgery, children undergoing chemotherapy treatments, premature babies trying to breathe with tiny lungs, or grandparents suffering from severe anemia. In as little as few minutes, you can become someones unnamed, unknown, but all-important hero. Saving a life is noble work that starts very simply and easily..

Understanding Your Blood Type

There are four major blood groups determined by the presence or absence of two antigens – A and B – on the surface of red blood cells. In addition to the A and B antigens, there is a protein called the Rh factor, which can be either present (+) or absent (–), creating the 8 most common blood types (A+, A-, B+, B-, O+, O-, AB+, AB-).

People who can donate blood.

Most people can give blood. You can give blood if you:

  • are mentally and physically fit and healthy.
  • weight is between 50kg to 160kg.
  • are between 17 and 66 (or 70 if you have given blood before)
  • are over 70 and have given blood in the last two years

People who can't donate blood.

You must not donate blood if you:

  • have history of Jaundice/Hepatitis.
  • have history of serious illness.
  • have a drug addiction.
  • have major surgery/expected surgery.
